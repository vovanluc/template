Course Overview:

In this exclusive masterclass Danny Mo Morris is going to change the way you approach creating bass lines forever. Not only will you discover the underlying relationship between harmony and groove, you’ll also learn that when creating your own bass lines – rhythm is king!

Within the masterclass Danny takes you step by step through the process of creating bass lines over 4 specific tracks and covers topics such as groove, timbre, bass-line creation, using chromatic approach notes and many more essential techniques every bass player should know.

Your Professor:

Known for his warm muted tone, Professor Danny “Mo” Morris has been playing bass for most of his life and has been an educator at the prestigious Berklee College of Music since 1988. He has taught thousands of students, many of whom have gone on to successful careers and perform around the globe. Danny has taught players of all levels and his mission as an educator is for his students to develop a solid rhythmic groove with their own distinct timbre, solidify their understanding of harmony, and discover the “art of playing bass on a song.”

5 Core Things you'll learn within this course

1 The power of using arpeggios within your bass lines - and exactly how they can be applied.

2 How you can use the “birds eye view” technique to change the way you approach creating bass lines.

3 How important it is to be locked in with the drummer - plus playing with and against the kick drum.

4 How to organise your bass lines using binary phrases.

5 Danny’s favourite muting technique and how you can apply it to your own bass lines.

Here’s a birds eye view of what you’ll be learning:

Session #1

  • How to match your bass line to the kick drum pattern.
  • The “Bird’s eye view” and how to make a unique contribution to a song.
  • Tonality bass ideas
  • How to use binary phrases within your bass lines.

Session #2

  • How to compose bass parts with harmony and arpeggios.
  • Building chords by number theory to organize bass lines.
  • Playing with the kick drum or being conversational.
  • How to create your own unique bass part.
  • Adding timbre (your sound).

Session #3

  • How to develop bass lines through improvising, jamming and organizing (creating something new)
  • Creating continuity by developing your organizational skills.

Session #4

  • How to play underneath a soloist.
  • Framing your bass lines by listening to what’s going on.
  • Playing a melody and the uses of repetition.
  • How to use pedal points (staying on one note through moving chord changes).
