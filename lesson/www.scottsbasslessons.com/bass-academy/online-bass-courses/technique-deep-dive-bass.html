Technique Deep Dive for Bass

Watch Course Introduction

Course Overview:

As a bass player having bad technique is like driving a car with a flat tire. You’re not going anywhere fast!

In this course Scott takes you through some of his best technique exercises in a step by step manner to make sure you are playing your bass cleanly, fluidly and not hindering your progress in any way.

A word of warning ;)…

Although some of the lessons within this course may seem relatively easy to begin with, don’t be fooled. These exercises could keep you busy for a lifetime, and if applied and practiced regularly will transform your bass playing. Feel free to watch the entire course, but afterwards we recommend focusing on just one exercise at a time until you can play it cleanly, fluidly and without thought.

10 Core Things you'll learn within this course

1 How to set your strap height so it’s perfect for you, and how to find the correct position.

2 Exactly how you should be positioning you fretting hand for ultimate effectiveness.

3 Multiple muting techniques and how to choose the right one for you.

4 The difference between rest strokes and free strokes, and why you should use them both.

5 Why you must choose between alternate and raking, and make a dedicated choice.

6 Exercises specifically designed to speed up the process of developing your technique.

7 Why you must be laser focused on you plucking hand - this is your engine.

8 How finger independence and slow controlled movements will speed up your development.

9 A cool string crossing exercise to develop your octave playing skills.

10 Why fretting hand muting is important as plucking hand muting, and how to focus on both.
